The Bigger Picture

FBLA at the National Level

Our chapter is one local link in an organization that spans the country.

Future Business Leaders of America began as an idea in 1937, when Columbia University professor Hamden L. Forkner proposed uniting the country's scattered high school business clubs into a single national organization. The first chapter was chartered in Johnson City, Tennessee, in 1942, and the organization has grown into the largest career and technical student organization of its kind, with members across thousands of chartered chapters nationwide today.

FBLA's stated mission is to inspire and prepare students to become community-minded business leaders in a global society through relevant career preparation and leadership experience. Nationally, the organization runs on three levels of competition — local chapter, then state leadership conferences like the one our delegates attend, and finally the National Leadership Conference, where top state qualifiers compete each summer in a different host city.

Beyond competitive events, FBLA members nationwide take part in the Business Achievement Awards program, build career portfolios, apply for FBLA-exclusive scholarships, and support March of Dimes as the organization's national community service partner.
